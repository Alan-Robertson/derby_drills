\subsection*{Spin Jab}
\label{drill:last_blocker/spin_jab}

This drill is about spinning out of an engagement with a blocker.
This is best setup as if there is only a single blocker remaining, with their back facing towards the jammer with both skaters initially stationary.  
 
The jammer should plant one toe stop, then reach across their body with the other leg, staying on their edges and threatening to pass the blocker.
The blocker should be unable to see the planted toe-stop and is forced to cover that trajectory to prevent the jammer from passing.

The jammer then performs a tight spin on their toe stop with their edge.
During this movement, the jammer should stay engaged, giving the blocker the feeling that they have successfully diverted the jammer's movement.
The push will also accellerate the spin, while slightly dragging the blocker out of position. 

Once the spin is complete the blocker should be moving in one direction, while the jammer should be facing and have momentum towards the other line.  
Alternatively, if the blocker hasn't fallen for the juke, the jammer can complete a full 360 degree spin, then continue in their original direction.
