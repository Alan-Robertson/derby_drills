\subsection*{Front Carve Juke}
\label{drill:last_blocker/front_carve}

This drill is about spinning out of an engagement with a blocker, with the blocker facing the jammer.
This is best setup as if there is only a single blocker remaining, chest blocking the jammer with both skaters initially stationary.  
 
The jammer should reach across their body, looking to put their left upper arm across the blocker's left upper arm, or their right upper arm across the blocker's right upper arm, while moving the matching leg across in the same direction.   
This threatens a simple passing move by suppressing the blocker's movement in that direction. 
The blocker is then forced to respond by moving to cover that trajectory, otherwise the jammer simply passes. 


Of course this response then opens up the other line, which we will seek to exploit.
The jammer's position is compromised by their engaged arm, which they should immediately disengage, while throwing a carve into toe-stop acceleration to head to the opposite line. 
To help clear the movement, dropping to a lower position will give more space to manoeuvre while opening the blocker's ribs for a passing hit with the shoulder. 


If the blocker has managed to correct their momentum in time, then the change in direction can be reversed, heading back to the initial line.
