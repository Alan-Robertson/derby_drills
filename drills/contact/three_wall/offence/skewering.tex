\subsection*{Drill: Skewering}
\label{drill:three_wall/skewering}

This drill requires four skaters, three blockers and an opposing offence. 

The blockers begin set up as a three-wall.
The offence's goal is to hit one of the butts into the other, then to launch into the brace. 

\begin{itemize}
\item Queue up from the inside or the outside of the track, lining the blockers up.
\item The initial hit into drive is to impart momentum onto the first blocker, allowing their own force to drive them into the second.
\item Once that blocker is no longer making contact with the offence, the next move is to take the brace.
\end{itemize}

The initial drive may be any of the hip, shoulder, chest or shelved drives.
When hitting the brace consider the advantages of either covering the same line as the initial approach, or setting up a threading situation.
