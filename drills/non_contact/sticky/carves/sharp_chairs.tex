\subsection*{Sharp Carves}
\label{drill:carves/sharp_chairs}

This is a jammer focused carving drill, with obstacles.
Light chairs are preferred to cones as they force good upper body placement.

Chairs should be placed in pairs around the track with one chair in lanes 2 or 3, and a second chair a few feet ahead in lanes 1 or 4 (start at 3-5 feet).

Skaters pass around the track at whatever speed they feel comfortable, passing around the first chair, then carving back in to the centre of the track to avoid the second chair.
Skaters should attempt to retain their momentum through this manoeuvre rather than burning off speed during the turn. 


The direction that the backs of the chairs face will modify the posture that the skaters take - start with the backs facing away from their path.

{\it Progressions:}
Skaters may manage their own progression by increasing their speed.
The drill may be made more difficult by decreasing the distance between the pairs of chairs, forcing a sharper carve. 
Lastly, rather than single chairs, chairs may be clustered forcing secondary or tertiary carves.
